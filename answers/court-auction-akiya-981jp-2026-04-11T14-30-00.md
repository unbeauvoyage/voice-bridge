### Price Range Analysis

From the 14 live Nara listings:
- Floor (cheapest): **¥330,000** (land-only, Gojo City, 684m²)
- Cheapest house: **¥1,380,000** (Kashihara, 52m² land)
- Mid-range houses: **¥1,770,000 – ¥2,800,000**
- Condominium: **¥2,020,000** (Kashihara)
- Urban commercial land (Yamato Takada): **¥6,300,000**

**Answer to the ¥2M–¥5M question:** Yes, this is realistic as a bid range for rural Nara houses. However, the *winning bid* (落札価格) typically exceeds 買受可能価額. From auction results (see Section 3), rural properties near reserve are common — a ¥1.77M reserve house could hammer at ¥1.8M–¥2.5M, while a desirable suburban lot could go 2–4x reserve. Winning a rural akiya for ¥1.5M–¥3M is plausible. ¥1M or below requires either no-bid rerun or agricultural land.

**Price relationship clarification:**
- 売却基準価額 (Standard sale price) = appraiser-set reserve, typically ~70% of market value
- 買受可能価額 (Minimum bid) = 80% of 売却基準価額 (the legal floor for bids under 民事執行法 Art. 60)
- 落札価格 (Hammer price) = what the winner actually pays; often exceeds reserve significantly in competitive cases

## 2. BIDDING PROCESS & TIMELINE (期間入札 / Sealed-Bid Auction System)

### Full Timeline from Publication to Title

| Stage | Description | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| 競売開始決定 (Auction commencement order) | Court accepts creditor's filing | Day 0 |
| 現況調査 / 評価 (Site inspection + appraisal) | Court-appointed appraiser visits | Weeks 0–8 |
| 公告 (Public notice) | Published in Official Gazette + BIT | ~8 weeks after commencement |
| 3点セット閲覧開始 (3-document set available) | BIT listing goes live | ~2 weeks after public notice |
| 入札期間開始 (Bid period opens) | Typically 8 days | ~3 weeks after 3点セット |
| 入札期間終了 (Bid period closes) | Last day to submit sealed bid | 8 days later |
| 開札 (Opening/counting of bids) | Single day; results published same day | 1 week after bid close |
| 売却許可決定 (Sale approval decision) | Court formally approves sale | ~1 week after 開札 |
| 確定 (Decision becomes final) | If no 執行抗告 (appeal) filed | 1 week after approval |
| 代金納付期限 (Payment deadline) | Full amount must be paid | Within 1 month of 確定 |
| 所有権移転登記 (Title transfer) | Automatic 嘱託登記 by court clerk | 1–2 weeks after payment |

**Total elapsed time from BIT listing to title: approximately 8–12 weeks.**

From BIT listing discovery to open-date (開札): the Nara examples show listings went live April 9, opening April 23 (14 days). So from public notice to bid close is effectively 3 weeks. The entire post-listing cycle to title is ~10 weeks.

Sources: [Sapporo District Court auction guide PDF](https://www.courts.go.jp/sapporo/vc-files/sapporo/file/r3_keibai_p06.pdf) | [競売手続きの流れ](https://saimu4.com/ninbai/3601/) | BIT schedule pages

### The 3点セット (Three-Document Set) — Physical Inspection Rule

The three documents (物件明細書 / 現況調査報告書 / 評価書) are available to download free from BIT during the bid period.

**Critical:** Buyers almost never get physical interior access before bidding. The 現況調査報告書 (current status investigation report) is based on the court bailiff/appraiser's visit, but dates from potentially 6–12 months prior to the listing going live. Buyers must make their bid decision entirely on:
1. The 3点セット documents
2. Exterior observation (self-conducted site visit)
3. Any publicly available registration/cadastral records

This is confirmed by the BIT site itself: physical inspection access is not provided as part of the auction process. This is one of the most significant risk factors versus private sales.

## 3. COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS

### Who Bids on Rural Akiya?

- **不動産業者 (Real estate professionals):** Dominant in urban auctions; present but less dominant for low-value rural akiya
- **個人投資家 (Individual investors):** Active for rural properties under ¥5M; barrier to entry is lower than perceived
- **競売代行業者 (Auction agent services):** Handle the process for individual buyers who can't navigate court procedures alone
- **リノベーション会社 / DIY buyers:** Growing segment for rural akiya specifically

### 競売代行 (Auction Agent) Fees

- **Investigation fee:** ¥33,000–¥55,000 per property (non-refundable even if you don't win)
- **Success fee:** 2.5%–3% of winning bid (no statutory cap — negotiable)
- Unlike standard real estate transactions, 仲介手数料 caps under 宅地建物取引業法 do not apply to auction representation fees; they are free-contract (準委任)

Source: [競売代行費用まとめ](https://kaitaideco.com/1075/)

**Key finding:** The very cheapest rural property (¥832K base, Yoshino County) hammered at just ¥856K — barely 3% above reserve. This is the low-competition rural akiya scenario. Meanwhile, desirable locations (Gose, Kashihara) go 2–7x reserve. **For sub-¥3M rural akiya, +10–20% over reserve is often sufficient to win; bidding +50% would be overkill and uneconomical.**

From Tokyo data (817 properties, 2015): ~5% sell below base, ~10% within 110% of base. Rural Japan shows similar low-competition dynamics at the extreme low end.

**8 of 14 properties in the March 2026 Nara Katsuragi batch received no bids** — these go to 特別売却 (special sale / first-come-first-served) at reserve price. This is a real channel: no-bid properties can be purchased at exactly 売却基準価額 after the auction period ends, without competitive bidding. Nara court confirmed special sales ran until January 2025.

### Individual Buyer Eligibility

Any natural person or legal entity may bid. Required at time of bid submission:
1. 陳述書 (Declaration that bidder is not a member of organized crime / 暴力団)
2. 住民票 (Resident certificate) or 資格証明書 (corporate certificate)
3. 宅地建物取引業者 (Licensed real estate agent) must also attach copy of license

No Japanese nationality requirement. Foreign residents with 住民票 are eligible.

## 4. LEGAL COMPLEXITY

### 引渡命令 (Delivery Order) — Court-Granted Occupant Removal

This is a mechanism unique to court auctions (民事執行法 第83条). After paying the purchase price, the buyer may apply to the same court for an order directing occupants to vacate. This avoids a separate civil lawsuit.

**Timeline from payment to vacant possession:**
1. Application to court: day 0 (must file within 6 months of payment; 9 months for certain occupants)
2. Delivery order issued: ~2–3 days after application
3. Order served on occupant: 2 days – 2 weeks
4. Appeal window: 1 week after service
5. If no appeal: order is final → apply for 強制執行 (forced eviction)
6. 強制執行 (execution): notice posted ~1 month before; actual eviction ~4–6 weeks after application

**Total from payment to forced eviction if contested: 2–4 months.**
If occupant leaves voluntarily (common with 不法占有 squatters or debtor-owners): 2–6 weeks total.

Source: [Mira-ie delivery order guide](https://investor.mira-ie.co.jp/blog/knowledge/there-are-tenants-in-house-bought-at-auction-delivery-of-property-and-compulsory-execution/)

### 占有者 (Occupant) Risk Types

| Occupant Type | Legal Protection | 引渡命令 Applicable? |
|---|---|---|
| 不法占有 (illegal squatter) | None | Yes — immediate |
| 抵当権後の賃借人 (tenant whose lease post-dates mortgage) | 6-month 明渡猶予 grace period | Yes — after 6 months |
| 抵当権前の賃借人 (tenant whose lease pre-dates mortgage) | Full lease survives auction | No — lease continues |
| Owner/debtor | No protection | Yes |

**Critical risk:** If a legitimate pre-mortgage tenant exists (rare in rural akiya, common in urban rentals), they cannot be evicted and the buyer inherits the tenancy. The 3点セット will disclose this; always verify.

Source: [Rakuten Bank tenant protection guide](https://www.shihou-anzai.com/other/keibai.html) | [借地借家法 明渡猶予](https://www.athome.co.jp/contents/words/term_1626/)

### Title Clearing — What Survives the Auction

Court auction under 民事執行法 extinguishes:
- **All mortgages (抵当権)** — cleared on title transfer
- **Most pledges and security interests** — cleared
- **General liens (一般先取特権)** — cleared

What **survives:**
- **法定地上権 (Statutory surface right):** If land and building were owned by different parties when the mortgage was created, the building owner retains a statutory right to use the land. This is a major trap — if you buy only the land at auction and a building exists on it belonging to someone else, they have legal ground use rights. Check the 3点セット carefully.
- **用益権 (Use rights):** 地上権 (surface rights), 地役権 (easements), and 永小作権 (perpetual cultivation rights) registered before the auction survive unless the court determines otherwise.
- **Qualifying leases:** Pre-mortgage leases (see above).

### 瑕疵担保責任 / 契約不適合責任 — No Warranty

Court auctions are 現況有姿 (as-is) sales. There is **no 瑕疵担保責任 (defect warranty)** and **no 契約不適合責任** (liability for non-conforming condition under the revised Civil Code). If the roof leaks, the structure has termites, or the foundation is cracked, the buyer has no recourse against the court or the debtor. Physical condition must be assessed from the 3点セット documents and exterior observation only.

### 固定資産税 (Fixed Property Tax) — Arrears Treatment

The prior owner's 固定資産税 arrears are **their debt, not the buyer's.** When the court sells the property, the proceeds are distributed to creditors (including the municipality for any tax claims). The buyer does not inherit arrears on the property itself.

**Exception — condominiums (マンション):** Unpaid 管理費 (HOA fees) and 修繕積立金 (repair reserve fund) can be inherited by the buyer under the 区分所有法. This is a real risk for condo auction purchases. Not typically applicable to standalone akiya.

Source: [競売の固定資産税精算](https://www.srp-re.jp/2023/08/03/%E7%AB%B6%E5%A3%B2%E3%81%AE%E5%9B%BA%E5%AE%9A%E8%B3%87%E7%94%A3%E7%A8%8E%E3%81%AE%E7%B2%BE%E7%AE%97%E3%81%AF%E3%81%A9%E3%81%86%E3%81%AA%E3%82%8A%E3%81%BE%E3%81%99%E3%81%8B/)

## 5. FINANCING (住宅ローン for Court Auctions)

### Deposit Requirement at Bid

- **保証金 (Bid deposit):** 20% of your planned bid amount (standard requirement; in practice typically ~20% of 売却基準価額 as a floor)
- Must be paid via **certified bank transfer** (振込保証書) to the court before bid submission
- If you win: deposit applies toward the purchase price
- If you lose: full deposit is returned within ~1 week
- **If you win but fail to pay the remaining amount by the deadline: deposit is forfeited** (重大なリスク)

### Mortgage on Court Auction Properties — Reality

**Technically possible, operationally difficult.**

**Flat 35 (フラット35 — government-backed fixed mortgage via 住宅金融支援機構):**
- Flat 35 *can* be used for court auction properties per Rakuten Bank FAQ
- **Critical constraint:** Property must pass a 適合証明 (conformance inspection) by a registered inspector, certifying it meets 住宅金融支援機構 structural and maintenance standards
- This inspection must be arranged **before bidding** (or as soon as possible after winning), but there is no guarantee it will pass
- If the property fails conformance inspection after you win: **deposit is forfeited** — there is no withdrawal right
- Rural akiya from 1970s–1980s will frequently fail Flat 35 standards (insulation, seismic standards)
- **Practical verdict: Flat 35 is usable for newer or structurally sound properties; risky for pre-1981 rural akiya**

Source: [Rakuten Bank Flat35 auction FAQ](https://help.rakuten-bank.net/faq/show/5731?category_id=340&site_domain=individual)

**Regular bank mortgages (住宅ローン 競売ローン):**
- Many major banks (MUFG, SMBC, Mizuho) decline court auction properties entirely due to inability to conduct pre-bid inspection
- Banks that are known to accommodate court auctions: **ARUHI / SBI新生銀行** (now partnered — SBIアルヒ took over SBI新生銀行 mortgage distribution from June 2025), some regional banks (地方銀行)
- **Timing constraint:** 代金納付 must occur within ~1 month of 確定. Mortgage disbursement must align exactly with this date. Banks must agree to a 代理納付 (proxy payment to court) mechanism — not all banks handle this
- Note: ARUHI merged/rebranded its SBI新生銀行 distribution function in 2025; confirm current 競売対応 products directly with SBIアルヒ

**Bottom line on financing:**
- **Cash buyers have no friction** — this is the natural channel for cash-rich buyers
- **Mortgage buyers face a sequential problem:** must get conditional pre-approval before bidding, then confirm final disbursement timing within 1 month post-award
- Rural akiya under ¥3M are within cash range for many buyers; this is probably the right lens (cash + renovation budget)
- A ¥5M+ suburban court auction property where mortgage is desired requires careful bank selection and timeline management

## 6. POST-AUCTION TO RENTAL

### Title Transfer (所有権移転登記)

- Happens via **嘱託登記 (court-instructed registration)** — the court clerk files the transfer directly with the Legal Affairs Bureau
- No buyer action required
- Timeline: **1–2 weeks after 代金納付 (payment)**
- At this point, the buyer legally owns the property

### Occupant Removal

If occupied (as in Example B — Uda City debtor-occupant):
- File for 引渡命令 immediately after payment
- If debtor cooperates: vacated in 2–4 weeks
- If contested: 2–4 months (see Section 4)
- Incentivized departure money (立退料) may accelerate voluntary exit; amounts vary but ¥100K–¥300K for rural properties is typical

### Physical Condition and Rental-Ready Timeline

Rural Nara akiya from 1970s–1977 will typically need:
- Roof inspection/repair: likely needed on 45–50 year old buildings
- Electrical rewiring to modern standards (required for habitability)
- Kitchen and bathroom modernization
- Potential foundation/seismic assessment
- Cosmetic work (cleaning, flooring, walls)

**Realistic rental-ready timeline:**
- Light renovation (property structurally sound): 3–6 months
- Full renovation (gut, new systems): 6–18 months
- Demolish + rebuild (land-value play): 12–24 months

Property management handoff (委託管理) can begin immediately after title transfer if building is habitable — but rural Nara akiya at ¥1.77M reserve will rarely be rental-ready without renovation.

## Bottom Line: Is Court Auction a Viable Channel?

### Verdict: Viable as a specialist channel, not a primary one

**Pros:**
1. **Genuine price floor** — ¥1.4M–¥2.5M for rural 5LDK houses with 250–475m² land is not achievable through SUUMO/HOME'S
2. **No competition from general buyers** — most private buyers are intimidated by the process; competition is lower than private market for rural sub-¥3M properties
3. **No-bid properties** (特別売却) are a real opportunity — ~40-60% of rural properties receive zero bids in a given auction cycle and go to first-come-first-served sale at reserve price
4. **Title is clean** — mortgages and liens extinguished by operation of law; no back-and-forth negotiation with multiple lien-holders
5. **3点セット is informative** — appraisal report is rigorous; often better documentation than private akiya sales

**Cons:**
1. **No interior access** — bidding blind on physical condition is a real risk for renovation-heavy purchases
2. **Deposit forfeiture risk** — getting cold feet after winning costs you 20% of bid amount
3. **Mortgage friction** — not impossible but requires extra preparation; cash is much simpler
4. **Occupant removal adds time** — if occupied, add 2–4 months before you can access or rent
5. **Volume is thin** — only 14 Nara properties in current cycle; specific location targeting is hard
6. **Rural market hammer prices** — while low-competition properties go near reserve, even "rural" results show 1.8–2.2x for anything with merit

**Recommended approach:**
- Use court auctions as a **secondary sourcing layer** — monitor BIT weekly for Nara/Wakayama listings
- Target specifically: properties with ¥0 bids that go to 特別売却 (can buy at reserve, no competition)
- Focus on: Yoshino County, Uda City, rural Gojo — where ¥1.5M–¥3M house-with-land is realistic
- Prepare for cash purchase or have mortgage pre-arranged with a 競売対応 bank (SBIアルヒ)
- Use a 競売代行業者 for first purchase (~¥50K + 3% success fee) to manage process risk
- Do not rely on court auctions for Nara City, Kashihara, or Yamato-Koriyama — competition is strong and prices approach private market levels

**Not a replacement for SUUMO/HOME'S** — private akiya via SUUMO offers physical inspection, negotiation, and standard mortgage. Court auction is a discount channel with process overhead. Both should run in parallel.
