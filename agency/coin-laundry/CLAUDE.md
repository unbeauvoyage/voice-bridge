## Mission
Build a complete, actionable business model for owning and operating a coin laundry (コインランドリー) in Kansai (Nara, Osaka suburbs, Wakayama, Kyoto). This is a **TYPE A/B passive income business** — customers do the work, machines earn money, owner visits 1-2 hours per day max.

## Why This Business
The CEO's target model: spend ¥150K–¥200K/month total costs (rent + utilities + part-time cleaning staff), earn ¥300K–¥500K/month revenue = ¥100K–¥300K passive monthly. A well-placed 10-machine laundromat near a university or dense residential area achieves this. Owner visits once or twice a day to clean and collect cash — no skill required.

## The Japanese Coin Laundry Market
- Japan has ~22,000+ coin laundries (growing at 2-3% per year)
- Structural demand: small Japanese apartments often lack space for home washing machines
- New driver: large-capacity industrial machines (comforters, futons) that home machines can't handle
- Franchise models (Wash House, Coinex, Tosei) are established and offer turnkey operation
- Independent operation is also viable in smaller markets (Nara, Wakayama towns)

## Priority Research Topics

### 1. The Business Model Numbers
- Monthly revenue for a 10-machine laundromat in: Nara city, Osaka suburb, Wakayama city
- Fixed costs: rent (what size space?), electricity (biggest cost — how much per machine?), cleaning staff, machine maintenance
- Is the ¥150K cost → ¥300K revenue model realistic? Find real operator data.
- Break-even timeline: how long from opening to profit?

### 2. Franchise vs Independent
- Wash House (ウォッシュハウス), Coinex (コインエックス), Tosei (東洗), White Laundry — compare models
- Franchise fee and royalty structure
- What support do they provide? Location selection? Machine supply? Marketing?
- Independent: can someone buy used commercial laundry machines and set up without a franchise?

### 3. Starting Capital
- New franchise: total investment including fit-out, machines, deposit
- Used machine route: commercial Panasonic/Toshiba/Aqua washer/dryer prices (new vs used)
- TYPE C ANGLE — Are there existing coin laundries for sale cheap? Owners retiring? Businesses failing post-COVID? Search tenpo.com, gyoumu-tenpo.com, j-net21 for laundry businesses for sale
- Minimum viable setup: what is the SMALLEST number of machines that makes this worthwhile?

### 4. Location Selection
- What area demographics work best? (students, families, no-washer households)
- What floor space is needed? (minimum viable)
- Nara city specifically — are there underserved residential areas? Where are existing laundries?
- Competition mapping: how close is too close to an existing laundry?

### 5. Operations — How Passive Is It Really?
- How many hours per day does an owner actually spend?
- What breaks? How expensive is machine repair?
- Can you hire one part-time cleaner and step back entirely?
- App-based payment systems (cashless) — which Japanese laundries use them? Does it reduce cash handling burden?

### 6. Legal
- Business license required? (Food Sanitation? No — laundry is simpler)
- Fire safety requirements for commercial dryers
- Zoning restrictions
- Can a foreigner own this under Business Manager Visa?
