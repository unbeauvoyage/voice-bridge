## Mission
Build a complete, actionable business model for owning and operating a vending machine route in Kansai (Nara, Osaka, Kyoto, Wakayama). This is a **TYPE A passive income business** — minimal capital, minimal owner involvement, machines earn money around the clock.

## Why This Business
The CEO's target model: spend ¥100K–¥150K/month total costs, earn ¥200K–¥300K/month revenue = ¥100K+ passive monthly with almost zero daily effort. A 3-5 machine route fits this model. A single well-placed machine at a tourist spot can earn ¥30K–¥80K/month with the owner restocking once a week.

## Priority Research Topics

### 1. The Business Model Numbers
- How much does a vending machine earn per month in Japan? (drinks, snacks, tobacco, phone accessories, etc.)
- What is the split between owner and location host? (typical: location gets 10-20% of revenue)
- What are realistic monthly revenues for machines in: tourist spots, train stations, office buildings, schools, hospitals?
- How often must machines be restocked? How long does a restock visit take?
- What is the profit margin on products sold?

### 2. Getting Started — Capital and Setup
- New machine cost vs used machine cost (Sanden, Fuji Electric, Panasonic Vending)
- Leasing a machine vs buying outright — which is better?
- Where to find used vending machines cheap (Yahoo Auctions, Junk dealers, operators retiring)
- TYPE C ANGLE: Are there operators exiting the business selling routes cheap? What does a "route" sale look like?

### 3. Locations — The Key Variable
- How to secure placement at a tourist location (Nara Park, Todai-ji area, Horyu-ji, Yoshino)
- What contracts do location hosts sign? Standard terms?
- Who owns the locations most likely to accept an independent operator? (vs Suntory/Kirin lock-in)
- Are there exclusive contracts that lock out independent machines? How to navigate?

### 4. Products — What Sells
- Drink machines vs snack machines vs specialty machines (tourist goods, phone accessories, capsule toys/gashapon)
- GASHAPON ANGLE: Capsule toy machines at tourist areas earn ¥30K–¥100K/month per machine with almost zero maintenance. This is extremely passive. Research this specifically.
- What products have best margin at tourist spots?

### 5. Legal and Operational
- Do you need a license to own vending machines in Japan? (食品衛生法, etc.)
- Electricity cost responsibility — who pays? (usually the location host)
- Tax treatment of vending machine income
- Can a foreigner own vending machines under a Business Manager Visa?

### 6. TYPE C — Exceptional Deals
- Retiring operators selling entire routes (machines + location contracts)
- Routes available below replacement value
- Bankrupt operators or estate sales with machines available
