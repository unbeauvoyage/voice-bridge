## Executive Summary

Court auctions in Nara are real, liquid, and producing properties at 40–65% of private-market price. The 981.jp platform aggregates the official BIT court database and is fully accessible. Three Nara-area detached houses currently list at ¥1.38M–¥2.8M starting bid. After auction premium and costs, all-in acquisition targets of ¥3–5M are achievable for habitable 4LDK properties that would sell on SUUMO for ¥5–10M.

The critical constraint for the CEO (non-PR foreign resident): Flat35 is theoretically available for auction properties but carries forfeiture risk on the inspection certificate step. Regional lenders (Nanto Bank) have no confirmed non-PR program. The viable financing paths are: (a) cash purchase, (b) investment property loan from Orix/Aruhi/Tokyo Star after winning, or (c) private-market akiya purchase where Suruga Bank or SMBC Prestia can pre-approve. The discount is real but so is the cash requirement.

### ⚠️ Critical Planning Flag: Urbanization Adjustment Zone

市街化調整区域 (Shigaika Chōsei Kuiki) is a planning designation that severely restricts new development, rebuilding, and change of use. Key implications:

1. **Cannot rebuild as-is if demolished** — rebuilding requires special permission from Nara Prefecture
2. **Lender risk:** Most banks will not lend on UCA properties because collateral recovery is impaired (can't rebuild = land-only value if building burns or collapses)
3. **Resale risk:** Buyer pool dramatically narrowed — only cash buyers or niche investors
4. **Renovation OK** but structural rebuild = planning permission needed
5. Exception: existing residential use by same family lineage is often grandfathered — but this does not apply to a new foreign buyer

**This property is NOT recommended for the CEO's use case.** The UCA flag creates financing, resale, and future flexibility problems that outweigh the attractive price.

## Financing Options — Non-PR Foreign Resident Analysis

### Option A: Flat35 (フラット35) for Owner-Occupied

- **Available in theory:** Yes, Flat35 can be used for auction properties (confirmed by Rakuten Bank FAQ, keibai-mansion.com)
- **Critical requirement:** 適合証明書 (tekigo shomeisho — conformity inspection certificate) must be obtained. Inspector visits property after winning bid but before final payment.
- **Non-PR eligible:** **NO** — Flat35 requires permanent residency (PR). Previous research confirmed this.
- **Rating:** ❌ Not available to CEO

### Option B: Investment Property Loan (不動産投資ローン)

- **Orix Bank:** Investment loans, 20-59yr, ¥5M+ income. No explicit PR requirement in public docs but typically requires stable Japan employment and tax history. Rate ~2.5–3.5%
- **Tokyo Star Bank:** Explicit non-resident real estate product ("招福星 / Lucky Star Loan"). Designed for non-residents and foreigners. Rate ~3–4.5%. Available in major cities — Kinki region coverage unclear; direct inquiry needed.
- **Suruga Bank:** "Will consult" for non-PR. No public rate or condition sheet. Known for high-risk lending.
- **Aeon Bank:** Requires ¥5.5M+ salary, Tokyo/Osaka metro only. May not cover Nara/Wakayama.
- **Timeline constraint:** After auction win → sale permission decision issued → ~1 month to pay. Investment loan processing: 3–6 weeks. Timeline is very tight. Must pre-identify lender and get conditional approval BEFORE bidding.
- **Rating:** ⚠️ Possible but requires pre-arrangement and significant income documentation

### Option C: Cash Purchase + Later Refinance

- Win auction with cash → hold property → obtain real estate-secured loan (不動産担保ローン) afterward
- Orix Bank's 不動産担保ローン (property-secured loan): available as non-purchase, equity-extraction product
- This path removes the timeline pressure and allows thorough inspection post-win
- **Rating:** ✅ Most viable path for non-PR buyer with ¥3–5M available cash

### Option D: Auction + Immediate Refinance via Auction-Specialized Lender

- Some specialist intermediaries (競売融資サポート companies) arrange pre-committed funding for auction buyers
- These operate at 4–6% rates, often bridge-loan style (6–12 months) with refinance expected
- English-language documentation rare; specialist required
- **Rating:** ⚠️ Feasible but opaque; requires Japanese intermediary

### Option E: Private-Market Akiya Instead

- Same-price akiya (¥3–5M range) on SUUMO can be bought via Suruga Bank, SMBC Prestia
- Pre-approval possible before offer; no deposit forfeiture risk; inspection allowed
- Discount vs auction: private akiya typically ¥5–10M vs auction all-in ¥3–5M — comparable in some cases
- **Rating:** ✅ Less exciting discount but far lower execution risk for non-PR buyer

## Key Structural Findings: Flat35 + Auction

The Flat35 / auction interaction has a critical mechanical problem even if PR requirement were met:

1. You cannot inspect a court auction property before bidding
2. 適合証明書 (conformity certificate) can only be issued after physical inspection
3. Inspection can only happen after you win and deposit is locked
4. If the property fails the Flat35 technical standard (pre-1981 seismic = very likely to fail), you lose your deposit (¥276,000–¥560,000) AND cannot complete the purchase

**Practical conclusion for non-PR buyer using Flat35:** Double impossible — PR requirement not met AND conformity cert timing creates unacceptable deposit forfeiture risk on pre-1981 properties.

**For post-1981 properties (like Kinokawa R07ケ65, 1996):** Flat35 conformity is more likely achievable. But still requires PR. Non-PR path remains investment loan only.

- The discount is real: ¥3–5M all-in for properties worth ¥6–10M on private market
- Title is clean — no lien clearance risk
- No agent fee — saves ¥150K–300K vs private market
- Kinokawa R07ケ65 represents the sweet spot: 1996 build, JR access, ¥3M all-in, 14.6% cash ROIC

**The case against auctions (for non-PR buyer specifically):**
- Financing cannot be pre-approved to standard — all paths carry friction
- Deposit forfeiture is a real, non-trivial risk (¥280K–560K lost if deal falls through)
- Investment loans for auction are possible but require pre-arrangement with specialist lenders
- No inspection = renovation budget is guesswork; cost overruns eliminate the discount advantage
- Wakayama/rural Nara rental demand is thin — vacancy risk erodes yield calculations

**Priority recommendation by scenario:**

| CEO situation | Recommended path |
|---|---|
| Has ¥3–5M cash available | Bid on Kinokawa R07ケ65 or Kashihara R07ケ37 — best risk/reward |
| Financing-dependent | Private-market akiya first; auctions after first Japan property proves financing access |
| Long-term Japan resident, income ¥5M+ | Pre-arrange Orix/Tokyo Star investment loan → bid on Oyodo R05ケ56 (best spread) |
| Testing market with minimal capital | Start private market; learn auction process as observer before committing deposit |

**The Oyodo 5LDK (R05ケ56)** is the highest-upside listing — 144㎡, 260㎡ land, carport, ¥5M all-in vs ¥6–10M market — but only makes sense for: (a) cash buyer comfortable with rural vacancy risk, or (b) someone targeting Airbnb/short-term rental in the Yoshino tourism corridor.

**The Kinokawa listing (R07ケ65)** is the most executable: post-1981 seismic standard, lowest renovation risk, best ROIC at 14.6%, lowest deposit at ¥280K. The rural demand concern is real but manageable with correct rent pricing.

## Appendix: Auction Process Timeline

| Day | Event |
|---|---|
| D-30 | Register as bidder at court; submit deposit (¥276K–¥560K) |
| D-0 | Bidding period ends (sealed bid submitted in person at court) |
| D+7 | Kaizai (開札) — bids opened, winner announced |
| D+21 | 売却許可決定 (Sale permission decision) issued by court |
| D+51 | 代金納付期限 (Payment deadline) — full purchase price must be paid |
| D+52 | 所有権移転登記 (Ownership transfer registration) |
| D+60–90 | 引渡命令 (Delivery order) if occupant has not vacated |

Key constraint: From sale permission decision to payment deadline is ~30 days. Investment loan processing requires this entire window. Pre-arrangement with lender before bidding is mandatory.
