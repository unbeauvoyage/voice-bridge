# Sarusawa Pond Coffee Shop Opportunity

Date: 2026-04-09

## Bottom line

Sarusawa Pond is viable for premium specialty coffee, but only if the concept is built for high tourist conversion and controlled capex. The location has enough demand to support an average of about 80 transactions/day at a blended ticket around `¥950`, which clears store-level operating break-even in a normal year. The weak point is not demand; it is capital efficiency. A heavy skeleton build in a heritage-style shell pushes payback out too far.

My conclusion:

- `Yes` for a premium specialty coffee concept with strong takeout, bilingual service, and local-product storytelling.
- `No` for a generic sit-down third-wave cafe that spends heavily on fit-out and competes head-on with ROKUMEI plus Starbucks.

## 1. Visitor Traffic

- Nara City recorded `14.87 million` visitors in 2024: `12.83 million` day-trippers and `2.04 million` overnight visitors.
- Nara Prefecture's north-area `A` zone, which includes Nara City, recorded `15.815 million` visitors in 2024.
- Sarusawa Pond sits inside the highest-traffic Nara Park / Kofuku-ji / Naramachi connector, so the north-area `A` zone is the best official proxy for site demand.

- For underwriting, I would use `¥230,000-280,000/month` for a realistic `20-25 tsubo` Sarusawa catchment site, not the cheapest portal comp

### Typical lease structure

Observed listing structure and standard small-shop practice suggest:

- `敷金`: `1-3 months`
- `礼金`: `1-2 months`
- `仲介手数料`: `1 month + tax`
- Guarantee company: often `0.8-1.0 month`
- Fire insurance / key exchange / signage approval / common-area rules: extra

Practical note:

- Prime tourist properties around Sarusawa Pond are scarcer than portal stock suggests, so the real “best frontage” deal may come with heavier deposits or less flexible food-use conditions.

### Available properties

What the current listings imply:

- There is active stock in the `20-27 tsubo` band around Kintetsu Nara / Naramachi / Sarusawa approach streets.
- Some stock is flagged `居抜き`, but online listings do not prove cafe-grade usable residuals. Power capacity, water, grease trap, exhaust, toilet condition, and facade constraints still need site inspection.

### Build-out cost

Current Japan fit-out guidance for cafes supports these ranges:

- `居抜き` refresh: about `¥200,000-350,000/tsubo`
- Standard cafe from light shell: about `¥300,000-500,000/tsubo`
- Heavier shell / historic building / stronger food prep: about `¥500,000-700,000/tsubo`

For a `22 tsubo` premium coffee shop near Sarusawa:

- Efficient `居抜き` case: `¥4.5m-¥8.0m`
- Normal specialty-cafe build: `¥7.0m-¥11.0m`
- Heritage-sensitive or shell-heavy build: `¥11.0m-¥15.0m+`

Equipment stack for a serious specialty shop:

- Espresso machine + grinders + water treatment: `¥2.0m-¥4.0m`
- Refrigeration / ice / dishwasher / batch brew / smallwares: `¥1.5m-¥3.0m`
- POS / furniture / signage / takeaway ware / opening stock: `¥1.0m-¥2.0m`

### Startup capital

Realistic total startup capital:

- `居抜き` / capex-disciplined case: `¥18m-¥24m`
- Full build / higher-design case: `¥25m-¥32m`

## 3. Competitive Landscape

Direct, clearly relevant coffee competition in the immediate catchment:

| Competitor | Approx relationship to site | Price read | Positioning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Starbucks Coffee Nara Sarusawaike | On the pond / direct landmark comp | Japan chain pricing; practical basket `~¥800-1,200` | Tourist-heavy, scenic, high throughput, brand trust |
| ROKUMEI COFFEE CO. NARA | Roughly station-side within the same walk-up catchment | Coffee and sets broadly `~¥560-1,200+` | Local flagship specialty coffee, destination reputation, beans/gifts |
| Anne-Marie Cafe | Nearby classic cafe catchment | Coffee around `¥660`, cafe au lait around `¥825`, sets much higher | Sit-down Western tea/cafe usage, slower dwell, more dessert/tea-led |
| Seattle's Best Coffee Sakura-dori area | Chain comp in the station approach zone | Lower-to-mid chain pricing | Convenience, commuter and quick stop |

Competitive take:

- The pond already has a strong chain operator in the exact scenic use case.
- The catchment already has a respected specialty operator in ROKUMEI.
- A new entrant should not be “another nice specialty coffee shop.”

### What is missing

The most defensible gap is:

- Independent premium coffee with `faster service than ROKUMEI`
- Strong `takeout-first` design for sightseeing flow
- Clear `Nara-local identity`: local sweets, deer/temple/Naramachi references handled tastefully, giftable beans or drip bags
- Bilingual menu, terrace/photo-friendly presentation, high iced-drink and seasonal-signature mix
- A pricing ladder that works for both tourists and repeat locals

What is not missing:

- Generic espresso drinks
- Generic sit-down cafe ambiance
- Generic third-wave branding

## 4. Financial Model

### Base-case assumptions

Model basis:

- Store size: `22 tsubo`
- Seats: `24-28`
- Format: premium specialty coffee + light pastry / dessert + beans / gift retail
- Rent + CAM: `¥260,000/month`
- Utilities: `¥95,000/month`
- Labor base: `¥780,000/month`
- Other fixed costs: `¥135,000/month`
- Total fixed costs: `¥1,270,000/month`
- Blended average ticket: `¥950`
- Variable cost ratio: `32%`
- Variable cost per transaction: about `¥304`
- Contribution margin: `68%`
- Stabilized average transactions/day: `80`

Recommended price ladder:

- Black coffee / batch / hand-drip: `¥650-780`
- Milk drinks: `¥700-850`
- Signature or seasonal tourist drinks: `¥820-980`
- Pastry / dessert add-on: `¥380-650`
- Local loyalty / morning set entry point: `¥780-880`

### Break-even

Store-level monthly operating break-even:

- Average month: about `65 transactions/day`
- February worst-case threshold: about `70 transactions/day`

This is a healthy ratio against the estimated footfall. At `10,800-13,000` estimated passers/day, break-even only needs roughly `0.5%-0.6%` pedestrian conversion.

Two different break-even views matter:

- Operating break-even: immediate in most months once the store reaches `65-70 tx/day`

Interpretation:

- The store can work operationally.
- The investment only works attractively if startup capital is kept close to the lower end of the range.

## 5. Seasonal Patterns

### Monthly variance

Using Nara City 2024 total visitor pattern:

- Strong months: `May (+15.6%)`, `Oct (+17.3%)`, `Nov (+18.9%)`
- Solid shoulder months: `Mar (+6.3%)`, `Apr (+8.4%)`
- Weak months: `Jan (-21.6%)`, `Feb (-23.2%)`, `Jul (-9.5%)`, `Sep (-9.0%)`

Using the more site-relevant north-area `A` data:

- Sarusawa/Nara Park corridor should materially outperform the city average in `May`, `Aug`, `Oct`, and `Jan`
- `August` is especially strong in the local area because of Nara summer event traffic

### Holiday effects

- Golden Week: very strong; May was `+34.7%` vs average in the `A` area
- Obon / Nara summer events: strong for this exact area; August was `+42.1%` in the `A` area
- New Year: citywide December is weak, but the north-area corridor rebounds in January because shrine/temple visitation supports the Sarusawa / Kofuku-ji side of town

### Weather effects

This section is an operating inference from Nara climate seasonality plus the tourism pattern:

- Rainy season reduces terrace usage and impulse purchases, so covered queue space matters
- Extreme summer heat cuts long dwell time but lifts iced drinks and takeout
- Winter lowers roaming impulse demand outside peak holidays, so hot drinks plus compact indoor comfort matter

Practical design implication:

- A shop here should bias toward quick indoor ordering, strong takeaway packaging, and some protected frontage rather than relying on long in-store stays

## Final recommendation

The location is viable for premium specialty coffee, but the winning format is not a pure “craft cafe.” It should be a scenic, high-throughput, locally branded specialty coffee shop that converts tourist flow efficiently while keeping a local-friendly morning offer.

The deal works if:

- Rent stays at or below roughly `¥280,000/month`
- Startup capital stays below roughly `¥24m-¥25m`
- The shop reaches `80 tx/day` at `¥950` average ticket
- The concept is visibly different from both Starbucks and ROKUMEI

The deal becomes weak if:

- Rent moves above `¥350,000/month`
- The site requires a heavy full-shell heritage build
- The concept depends on long sit-down dwell rather than tourist conversion
