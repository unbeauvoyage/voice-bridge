# Nara Coffee Shop: Equipment Sourcing and Critical Path to Opening

Date: 2026-04-09

## Bottom line

For a Nara / Sarusawa coffee shop, the opening date is usually **not** controlled by the food-service permit itself and usually **not** by POS.

The date is controlled by:

1. **Whether the space is already cafe-capable**
2. **How much plumbing / electrical / exhaust work is needed to make the site inspection-ready**
3. **Whether the espresso machine and custom fixtures are stocked or `受注発注`**

If you force this to one item, the longest-lead item that determines opening date is:

- **the space becoming inspection-ready**, not the permit paperwork

Among equipment only, the longest-lead item is:

- **the 2-group espresso machine**, if you choose a premium or `受注発注` model

My read for Sarusawa:

- **Best case** (`居抜き`, vacant, food-use ready, stocked equipment): `6-8 weeks` from lease signing to opening
- **Base case** (older small-shop stock, light utilities work, standard commercial equipment): `8-12 weeks`
- **Slow case** (heavy MEP work, older machiya/shell issues, custom millwork, made-to-order machine): `14-18+ weeks`

So the prompt's `16-20 weeks` is **not** the default case. It is a plausible downside case.

## 1. Current sourcing timeline by category

This section combines live supplier signals with practical restaurant-opening sequencing. Equipment stock moves fast, so treat this as a current market read, not a guarantee.

### Order now: what is available immediately vs. what requires wait?

Available immediately or close to it:

- stock commercial grinders
- used / refurbished equipment from secondary dealers
- basic tablet POS
- off-the-shelf undercounter refrigeration and shelving

Usually requires waiting:

- premium 2-group machines
- custom-color or custom-spec machines
- machines that need distributor import allocation
- made-to-order refrigeration or ice units
- custom counters, built-in cabinetry, facade signage packages

### Practical takeaway

If the opening date matters more than prestige, do **not** let the machine choice drift into `受注発注` after the lease is signed. Lock the machine and grinder package in the same week the site is measured.

## 2. Lease negotiation timeline

For a small Nara tenant deal, the practical path is:

- property selection and offer / application: `3-7 days`
- landlord review / guarantor review: about `3-10 days`
- contract preparation, signatures, deposit, first payment: about `5-10 days`
- handover: sometimes immediate for vacant stock, sometimes `相談`

Realistic planning range:

- **fast deal**: `1-2 weeks`
- **normal deal**: `2-4 weeks`

This matches the prompt well enough.

### Access to space

Access is **not always immediate** after signing. In current local stock, some Nara listings show immediate handover, while others show negotiable handover timing. For Sarusawa / Naramachi stock, assume access is only certain once:

- contract is fully signed
- deposit and first invoices are paid
- any landlord fit-out conditions are cleared

## 3. Buildout timeline

## Minor buildout

If you take a second-generation cafe / food-capable space:

- paint, lighting, shelving, minor sink / counter work, small electrical corrections: `2-4 weeks`

## Major buildout

If the space needs meaningful plumbing, exhaust, electrical capacity, grease handling, toilet correction, or facade coordination:

- construction alone: `4-8 weeks`
- realistic lease-to-inspection-ready path: more like `6-10 weeks`

## Sarusawa buildout complexity

My read is **moderate-to-high**, but not automatically extreme.

Why it is harder than a generic suburban tenant:

- older building stock is common
- tourist-frontage spaces can have facade / signage sensitivity
- Nara City has landscape and outdoor-sign controls that matter more in historic-core areas
- if the unit is not already food-use ready, electrical / water / exhaust surprises are common

Why it is not automatically worst-case:

- your target size from prior Sarusawa analysis is only about `20-25 tsubo`
- a small second-generation unit can avoid the longest construction path
- if you choose an actual `居抜き` or cafe-capable space, much of the timing compresses

Conclusion:

- **low complexity** only if the site is already food-use ready
- **base-case Sarusawa** is **moderate**
- **high complexity** if you are converting a machiya, office, or retail shell that lacks restaurant utilities

## 4. Licensing path: what actually runs in parallel?

The biggest correction to the prompt is here.

### Food-service permit timing

As of the Nara City page updated **2025-06-02**, the city instructs applicants for restaurant-type licenses to apply **`2-3 weeks before` the desired opening date**, not `8-12 weeks`.

That means:

- the permit itself is **not** usually the longest path
- the real dependency is getting the site ready for inspection

### What must be ready before food permit submission?

Nara City requires:

- kitchen / facility drawings
- overall layout
- food sanitation manager qualification
- corporate registry if the operator is a corporation

You can prepare those during design and buildout.

### Can permit work run parallel with buildout?

Yes, partially.

Best sequencing:

- pre-consult with health office during design
- finalize layout during buildout
- submit the formal application about `2-3 weeks` before opening
- schedule inspection for when the site is materially complete

The permit cannot finish before the facility is ready, but the paperwork and consultation can overlap with construction.

### Fire / opening notification

As of the Nara City fire page updated **2024-07-18**, a `防火対象物使用開始届` should be submitted **`7 days before`** use begins. The fire department also states that the inspection date is adjusted when the notice is filed.

This is another sign that the final bottleneck is the site reaching inspection-ready condition.

### Business registration

This is **not** normally critical path.

- For a sole proprietor, the National Tax Agency's current procedure guidance does **not** make the opening notice a pre-opening filing; it is filed after business start.
- For a corporation, Ministry of Justice guidance says fully online incorporation registration can be processed within `24 hours` when the qualifying conditions are met, and tax filings come after registration.

In practice:

- `sole proprietor`: effectively non-blocking for opening
- `corporation`: allow `1-2 weeks` for practical setup, but it can be started before lease signing

## 5. Staffing and training

Using the current Nara labor market read already developed in this workspace, staffing is a real workstream but not usually the opening bottleneck unless you insist on a fully bilingual launch team from day one.

Practical ranges:

- recruit lead barista / opening staff: `2-4 weeks`
- recruit part-time support: `1-3 weeks`
- menu and service training: `2-3 weeks`
- equipment-specific dialing and service rehearsal: last `1-2 weeks` before opening

### Can training overlap with licensing?

Yes.

The clean sequence is:

- recruit during construction
- train on SOPs, service scripts, POS, and menu before equipment arrives
- do live equipment training once machine install is complete
- use the final inspection window and soft-opening week for workflow practice

## 6. Critical path analysis

## Day 0 = lease signed

What runs in parallel immediately:

- final measured survey
- equipment orders
- contractor mobilization
- POS account / payment onboarding
- recruiting
- branding / signage approvals
- food-sanitation-manager course if still needed

What does **not** run fully in parallel:

- final health permit issuance
- fire sign-off tied to actual use of the completed space
- staff training on the installed machine

## Critical path by scenario

### Scenario A: cafe-capable `居抜き`

Critical path:

`Lease signed -> minor buildout -> equipment install -> permit inspection -> soft open`

Likely opening window:

- `6-8 weeks`

Likely bottleneck:

- whichever is slower between:
  - finishing the site for inspection
  - receiving/installing the espresso machine

### Scenario B: older Sarusawa shell / major MEP work

Critical path:

`Lease signed -> utilities/design resolution -> major buildout -> equipment install -> permit/fire inspection -> open`

Likely opening window:

- `10-14 weeks` base
- `14-18+ weeks` if machine/custom fixtures are also long-lead

Likely bottleneck:

- **site readiness**, especially plumbing / electrical / exhaust correction

## What actually determines opening date?

My answer:

- **Base-case Sarusawa**: `buildout + inspection readiness`
- **Equipment-only answer**: `2-group espresso machine`

If the site is already truly cafe-ready, the espresso machine becomes the most likely single long-lead item. If the site is not cafe-ready, buildout dominates everything.

## 8. How parallel work accelerates the schedule

The opening date moves earlier if you do these before or immediately after lease:

- choose a site that is already food-use ready
- choose a stocked machine, not a prestige `受注発注` machine
- use off-the-shelf refrigeration and shelving
- start recruitment before construction finishes
- start payment onboarding before the POS hardware arrives
- pre-consult the health office during layout design, not after construction
- avoid custom counters unless they materially improve revenue

The opening date slips when you do these late:

- final machine selection after construction starts
- menu design after staffing starts
- signage / facade approval after fabrication starts
- QR payment applications in the last week before opening

## 9. What can start before lease is signed?

Safe to start before lease:

- entity choice and tax setup planning
- bank / payment provider setup
- food sanitation manager course booking
- equipment quoting and shortlisting
- grinder / machine decision
- contractor bidding
- staffing pipeline and lead-barista recruiting
- menu development
- branding and bilingual service scripts
- landlord-ready application pack: ID, financials, guarantor docs, business plan

Can start before lease, but only if the site brief is tight:

- reserving a machine allocation
- ordering grinders
- ordering POS hardware

Do **not** fully commit before measured site confirmation:

- custom counters
- exact refrigeration sizes
- plumbing-dependent equipment
- final signage fabrication

## Final answer

If the question is "what determines opening date?", the answer is:

- **the space reaching inspection-ready condition**

If the question is "what is the single longest-lead purchased item?", the answer is:

- **the 2-group espresso machine**

For Sarusawa, the fastest route is:

- take a real `居抜き` or food-ready site
- order a stocked commercial machine immediately after lease
- keep refrigeration and fixtures off-the-shelf where possible
- run recruiting, POS onboarding, and permit prep in parallel with buildout

That is how you get closer to `6-8 weeks` instead of drifting into `14-18+ weeks`.
