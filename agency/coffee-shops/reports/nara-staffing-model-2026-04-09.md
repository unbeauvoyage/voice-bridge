# Nara Coffee Shop: Staffing Model and Operational Requirements

Date: 2026-04-09

## Bottom line

For a Sarusawa Pond coffee shop, the launch staffing model should be `owner-operated`, not `passive-owner`.

- Recommended customer hours: `08:00-20:00` daily
- Prep window: `07:00-08:00`
- Standard launch team: `1` owner/head barista, `3` regular part-time baristas, `1` weekend/seasonal floater, and `contract` deep cleaning
- Normal-month all-in labor cost: about `¥767,000/month`
- Low-season flexed labor cost: about `¥704,000/month`
- Peak-month labor cost: about `¥827,000/month`

Using the existing Sarusawa operating case in this workspace of about `80 tx/day` at a `¥950` average ticket, labor runs about `33.6%` of average-month sales. That is workable, but it is not loose. The model improves materially once the shop reaches about `90 tx/day`, or the average ticket moves closer to `¥1,050`.

## Working assumptions

This report uses the operating assumptions already established in the Sarusawa market work:

- Sarusawa base case: about `80 transactions/day`
- Blended average ticket: about `¥950`
- Base monthly sales in an average month: about `¥2.28m`
- Store format: premium specialty coffee plus light pastry/dessert and gift retail
- Sarusawa demand shape: strongest `13:00-16:00`, with smaller peaks at `09:00-11:00` and `16:30-18:30`

Where this report is making an inference rather than citing an official data series, I say so explicitly.

## 1. Store hours and coverage

### Recommended hours

Recommended launch hours for Sarusawa Pond:

- `08:00-20:00` daily for customers
- `07:00-08:00` prep by the opener
- Optional `20:00-21:00` extension only on `Fri-Sun`, holidays, and event weeks once the store proves evening demand

Why:

- The core Sarusawa tourist flow is late morning through late afternoon, not commuter-first.
- Official nearby competitor hours cluster around this band:
  - Starbucks Coffee Nara Sarusawa-ike: `08:00-21:00`
  - ROKUMEI COFFEE CO. NARA: `08:00-18:00`
- A `07:00` open is usually too early for the Sarusawa demand mix unless the concept deliberately targets breakfast sets and local repeat traffic.

My operating recommendation:

- Start at `08:00-20:00`
- Extend to `21:00` only after at least `2` consecutive strong weeks above about `90 tx/day`, or during cherry blossom, Golden Week, Tokae, October-November foliage, and New Year temple-visit periods

### Coverage by daypart

This is an operating inference from the Sarusawa traffic pattern and nearby attraction timing.

| Time band | Weekday coverage | Weekend / holiday coverage | Read |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| `07:00-09:00` | `1` | `1` | Prep and soft open; solo is fine |
| `09:00-11:00` | `2` | `2` | Morning sightseeing and takeaway lift |
| `11:00-12:00` | `1-2` | `2` | Transitional band |
| `12:00-16:00` | `2` | `3` | Core peak; strongest staffing need |
| `16:00-18:30` | `2` | `2-3` | Sunset/photo traffic and dessert/takeout |
| `18:30-20:00` | `1` | `1-2` | Usually solo on weekdays; keep extra help on event nights |

### Solo vs double coverage

Solo operation is acceptable only in these windows:

- `08:00-09:00`
- `18:30-20:00` on normal weekdays
- Some winter weekdays between `11:00-12:00` if the menu is simple and pastry handling is light

Double coverage is the minimum standard for:

- `09:00-11:00`
- `12:00-18:30`

Triple coverage is justified for:

- `12:00-16:00` on weekends and national holidays
- `13:00-16:00` in March-May, August event weeks, October-November, and New Year corridor peaks

## 2. Positions needed

### Launch staffing plan

| Position | Count | Type | Core responsibility |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Head barista / owner | `1` | Full-time | Open, quality control, ordering, training, admin, primary floor lead |
| Assistant baristas | `3` | Part-time regular | Drink production, register, pastry plating, close cleaning |
| Weekend / seasonal floater | `1` | Part-time | Peak support, language coverage, vacation fill-in |
| Cleaner | `0` full-time | Contracted | Deep clean only; daily cleaning stays in-house |

### Head barista / owner

Recommendation:

- The owner should be `full-time on the floor` for at least the first `12` months.
- Do not underwrite launch economics with a passive owner plus a hired full-time manager unless revenue is already proven much higher.

Reason:

- Sarusawa is seasonal and tourist-heavy.
- The staffing base is affordable with one owner-lead.
- A separate hired store manager adds roughly `¥323,000/month` all-in, which pushes the labor base to about `¥1.09m/month`.
- At that cost, the shop needs about `¥3.63m/month` in revenue, or about `127 tx/day` at a `¥950` ticket, just to hold labor near `30%` of sales.

### Cashier / front-of-house

At launch, this should be a `barista dual role`, not a standalone position.

- Up to about `100-110 tx/day`, a separate cashier is not necessary if the menu is streamlined and POS flow is clean.
- Above that level, the first extra hire should usually be a `peak FOH / runner` for `4-6` hours, not a permanent all-day cashier.

### Cleaning and prep

Recommendation:

- Daily prep and close cleaning: in-house
- Deep cleaning: contract `1-2` times per week
- No full-time prep or cleaner at launch

This stays efficient until:

- food production becomes heavier, or
- daily transactions move well above `110-120`, or
- the shop adds a larger seated food program

## 3. Skill requirements

### Coffee expertise

The shop needs `specialty-intermediate`, not competition-level, coffee skill.

One person must be able to:

- dial in espresso and adjust grind through the day
- maintain milk texture consistency
- troubleshoot extraction and workflow
- train juniors on recipe and speed standards

Assistant baristas do not all need deep specialty backgrounds. For a Sarusawa store, it is better to hire for:

- calm service under tourist rushes
- clean workflow
- reliability
- ability to learn a fixed drink system quickly

### English ability

English is not optional at this location. It is commercially useful.

Why:

- Nara City reported about `20.0%` international visitors in 2024
- Sarusawa should skew even more tourist-facing than the city average because it sits inside the main sightseeing corridor

Practical requirement:

- `1` lead with strong conversational English
- at least `1-2` part-time staff who can handle ordering, allergy/basic ingredient questions, and simple local recommendations in English

My pay recommendation is to treat English as a `real hiring premium`, not as a nice-to-have.

### Customer service and POS

Training baseline should include:

- line management during tourist surges
- short bilingual service scripts
- refund/remake policy
- allergy and ingredient answers
- upsell script for pastry and gift items
- POS proficiency in `Square`, `AirREGI`, or equivalent tablet POS

## 4. Wage expectations in Nara

### Current legal floor

- Nara Prefecture minimum wage: `¥1,051/hour`
- Effective date: `2025-11-16`

### Market pay read

Current Nara-area cafe listings and specialty coffee postings suggest these launch ranges are realistic:

| Role | Recommended pay band | Read |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| General cafe part-time | `¥1,100-1,150/hour` | Slightly above floor; needed to recruit reliably |
| Strong part-time barista | `¥1,150-1,200/hour` | Better for tourist-zone speed and reliability |
| Bilingual or senior part-time | `¥1,180-1,250/hour` | English plus floor independence |
| Full-time barista | `¥220,000-240,000/month` | Fits local specialty and chain-adjacent market |
| Full-time lead / head barista | `¥240,000-290,000/month` | More realistic for a tourist-zone lead |
| Store manager / absentee-owner replacement | `¥280,000-320,000+/month` | Only works at materially higher revenue |

### Premium above minimum wage

Recommended underwriting premium over Nara minimum wage:

- Coffee-skill premium: about `+¥50-120/hour`
- English-service premium: about `+¥50-150/hour`
- Weekend closer or peak-event premium: about `+¥30-50/hour` or a fixed shift allowance

### Part-time vs full-time differential

Part-time hourly pay can be close to the market floor, but full-time cost is higher because of:

- schedule stability
- training responsibility
- opening/closing responsibility
- employer social insurance
- paid leave and retention expectations

### Benefits

Recommended minimum package:

- commuter reimbursement
- one shift drink / light meal benefit
- paid training time
- quarterly coffee training budget
- social insurance for eligible full-time staff

Important legal nuance:

- If the shop is incorporated, full-time employees are generally inside standard social insurance rules.
- If it remains an individual proprietorship in a service trade such as a restaurant/cafe, compulsory health and pension enrollment can differ.
- For recruiting and retention, I would still underwrite the lead role as insured.

This is the recommended launch staffing cost for a normal month.

Notes:

- Employer burden above assumes roughly `15.37%` on the insured lead role before age-`40-64` care-insurance add-ons.
- If the lead falls into the `40-64` care-insurance band, add roughly another `0.81%` employer cost on that insured compensation.
- If the owner does not pay themself a salary during ramp-up, the cash payroll outlay is lower, but that understates the real operating economics.

### Seasonal labor flex

| Month type | Labor cost | How |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Low season | about `¥704,000` | Cut part-time hours by about `15%`; keep owner lead full-time |
| Normal month | about `¥767,000` | Standard base roster |
| Peak month | about `¥827,000` | Add `¥60,000` of part-time coverage for weekends, holidays, and event windows |

### Turnover

This is an operating benchmark, not an official Nara dataset.

- Lead role turnover target: below `15%`
- Part-time turnover expectation: `30-45%` annually
- Whole-team planned annual turnover: about `30-35%`

In practice, that means budgeting for:

- `1-2` part-time replacements per year
- recurring training and paid shadow shifts

## 6. Operational efficiency

### Throughput assumptions

At the current Sarusawa base case:

- Average day: about `80 tx/day`
- Average month: about `2,400 transactions`
- Average open-hour throughput: about `6.7 tx/store hour`

Practical throughput targets:

- Normal weekday peak: `8-10 tx/store hour`
- Weekend / holiday peak: `10-12 tx/store hour`
- Short burst capacity with streamlined drinks: `12-15 tx/store hour`

### Productivity metrics

Recommended benchmarks:

- Overall transactions per paid labor hour: about `4.5-5.0`
- Peak-window cups per barista per hour: about `8-10`
- Short burst cups per barista per hour: about `12-14` if the menu is skewed to batch coffee and simple espresso drinks

### Labor cost per transaction

Normal month:

- `¥766,700 / 2,400 transactions = about ¥319 per transaction`

This is a useful control metric. If it starts drifting above `¥330-340/tx` outside of winter, staffing or menu flow is too heavy for the sales base.

## 7. Break-even impact

Using the existing Sarusawa operating case in this workspace:

- Average month sales: about `¥2.28m`
- Normal-month labor cost: about `¥766,700`
- Labor as a share of average-month sales: about `33.6%`

Seasonal read:

- February-type month at `¥1.63m` sales:
  - base roster: about `47.0%` labor
  - flexed roster: about `43.2%` labor
- Peak `¥2.72m-2.76m` month:
  - about `27.8%-28.2%` labor at the standard base
  - about `30.4%` labor if extra peak coverage is added

### Revenue needed for healthier labor ratios

To hold labor near common cafe targets:

- `30%` labor ratio needs about `¥2.56m/month`
- `28%` labor ratio needs about `¥2.73m/month`

At current pricing, that implies:

- about `90 tx/day` for `30%` labor at a `¥950` ticket
- about `96 tx/day` for `28%` labor at a `¥950` ticket
- only about `81 tx/day` for `30%` labor if the average ticket is raised to `¥1,050`

The operational implication is straightforward:

- If the shop wants to stay around `80 tx/day`, it should push ticket mix upward with pastry, dessert, beans, and gift items.
- If the shop wants to stay at a `¥950` ticket, it needs a clearer path to `90+ tx/day`.

## 8. Sourcing strategy

### Where to recruit

Priority channels:

- Hello Work Nara for general local recruitment
- major job boards such as Indeed and JobBox for part-time volume
- local hospitality operators around Kintetsu Nara, Naramachi, and Nara Park for experienced service staff
- direct outreach to specialty coffee staff already working in Nara and Kyoto who may want a stronger concept or more stable shifts

### English-speaking talent

Best practical sources:

- hotels, guesthouses, visitor-facing retail, and tourism operators in the Nara Park / Kintetsu Nara corridor
- current or former cafe staff from tourist-facing chains
- bilingual Japanese returnees or working-holiday residents

The key hiring logic in Sarusawa is:

- hire service and language first
- train fixed coffee workflow second

That is usually faster than trying to find fully formed specialty baristas with strong English in Nara.

### University partnerships

The most relevant local student pools are:

- Nara Women's University
- Nara Prefectural University

These are useful for:

- weekday lunch cover
- weekend shifts
- language-capable part-timers

My recommendation is to use universities for `supplemental part-time labor`, not for the core opening/closing backbone.

### Visa and work-permit considerations

This is a legal-risk area. Use caution.

- Students with `資格外活動許可` can generally work up to `28 hours/week`
- That makes them useful for part-time shifts, but not for your core full-time coverage
- The cleanest foreign-hiring pool is usually people with unrestricted or broad work rights, such as permanent residents, spouses, long-term residents, or working-holiday status

Do not build the plan around sponsoring ordinary barista roles under `技術・人文知識・国際業務` without immigration review. Routine cafe service is a weak fit for that status unless the role is structured around a genuinely specialized international-service function.

## 9. Scaling plan

| Sales / traffic stage | Staffing model | Read |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Up to `70 tx/day` | Owner lead plus `2-3` part-timers | Keep one-person edges and two-person peaks only |
| `80-95 tx/day` | Current recommended launch model | `3` regular part-timers plus `1` floater |
| `95-110 tx/day` | Add `80-100h/month` senior support | Usually the floater becomes a near-regular or senior shift lead |
| `110-130 tx/day` | Add dedicated peak FOH / runner or prep support | Needed before adding a full all-day cashier |
| Above `130 tx/day` or larger food menu | Split bar, FOH, and prep more clearly | This is the point where a second full-time lead becomes viable |

Practical rule:

- Keep the launch model `owner-led`
- Add `peak support` first
- Add a `second full-time lead` only after traffic is sustained, not just because weekends feel busy

## Final recommendation

For Sarusawa Pond, the correct opening staffing model is:

- `08:00-20:00` daily customer hours
- `1` full-time owner/head barista
- `3` regular part-time baristas
- `1` weekend / seasonal floater
- no dedicated cashier at launch
- no full-time cleaner at launch

Under current 2026 labor conditions in Nara, that is a roughly `¥767,000/month` labor base and stays close to the existing Sarusawa break-even model already in this workspace.

The main operating risk is not whether the shop can be staffed. It can. The real question is whether the store gets to either:

- about `90 tx/day`, or
- an average ticket closer to `¥1,050`

without losing the tourist-conversion advantage of the site.
