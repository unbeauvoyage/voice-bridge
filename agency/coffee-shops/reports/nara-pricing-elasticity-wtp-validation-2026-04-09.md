# Nara Coffee Shop: Pricing Elasticity and Willingness-to-Pay Validation

Date: 2026-04-09

## Bottom line

`¥650` is a realistic blended average transaction assumption for a Sarusawa Pond coffee shop, but only as a `base-case beverage-led basket`, not as an aggressive premium case.

My read after checking current Nara and Kyoto tourist-zone pricing plus official tourism-spend data:

- Defensible stabilized average ticket range: `¥620-720`
- Best single underwriting point today: `¥650-680`
- `¥750-800` is possible only with meaningful pastry / dessert attachment or a larger premium-signature mix
- The older `¥950` average already used elsewhere in this workspace is too high for a drink-led model and should be treated as a food-plus-retail case, not a default coffee-only case

The most important operational implication is this:

- `¥650` validates customer willingness to pay
- it does **not** validate the earlier `80 tx/day` economics under the existing cost model
- at the fixed-cost and margin assumptions already used in this repo, the shop needs about `96 tx/day` to break even at a `¥650` ticket

## 2. Tourist pricing elasticity

### Official spending capacity

From the 2024 Nara City tourism report:

- average day-trip visitor spend: `¥4,938`
- average day-trip food and drink spend: `¥1,603`
- average lodging visitor spend: `¥31,754`
- average lodging visitor food and drink spend: `¥7,282`

That matters because a Sarusawa Pond coffee purchase sits inside an already-large food-and-drink wallet:

- `¥650` drink = about `41%` of the average day-trip food-and-drink spend
- `¥800` drink = about `50%`
- `¥1,100` drink + pastry = about `69%`

So tourists can absorb `¥650-800` comfortably. Elasticity gets meaningfully steeper only once the visit starts feeling like a meal replacement rather than a coffee stop.

### Foreign tourists

The Japan Tourism Agency's 2024 inbound survey shows large differences in total trip spend by nationality, but once food spend is normalized by length of stay, daily food budgets overlap more than headline totals suggest.

Examples for `観光・レジャー目的` travelers:

- United States: food `¥75,180`, average stay `10.6` nights, about `¥7,100/day`
- United Kingdom: food `¥84,739`, average stay `12.6` nights, about `¥6,700/day`
- Australia: food `¥84,749`, average stay `13.7` nights, about `¥6,200/day`
- China: food `¥46,971`, average stay `6.6` nights, about `¥7,100/day`
- Taiwan: food `¥38,560`, average stay `5.4` nights, about `¥7,100/day`
- Hong Kong: food `¥52,960`, average stay `6.2` nights, about `¥8,500/day`
- Korea: food `¥28,655`, average stay `3.6` nights, about `¥8,000/day`

Inference:

- English-speaking markets do spend more per trip overall
- but daily food budgets overlap heavily with Asian markets
- so I do **not** see evidence for separate list pricing by origin

What I do see:

- foreign tourists from English-speaking and European markets are more likely to reward story, quality, and aesthetics with a higher accepted ticket
- Asian tourists are not necessarily lower-budget on a per-day basis, but they are more likely to compare value across nearby options and react to whether the drink is photogenic, local, and convenient

### Domestic tourists

Domestic tourists in Nara are premium-tolerant only when the offer is clearly linked to place:

- scenic view
- Nara-local ingredients
- high-quality packaging
- seasonal / photo-friendly signatures

For domestic leisure visitors, `¥650-780` is defensible. Plain coffee above about `¥700` is harder unless the setting and presentation are visibly better than nearby alternatives.

### Experience value vs coffee quality

The Kyoto comps suggest tourists are paying for both, but not equally.

The pricing gap between ROKUMEI Nara and Bread, Espresso & Arashiyama Garden is too large to be explained by bean quality alone. The uplift is coming from:

- scenic location
- tourism convenience
- bilingual / visitor-friendly presentation
- Instagram value
- the feeling of buying a Kyoto / Nara memory, not just caffeine

That is directly relevant to Sarusawa Pond. A Sarusawa concept can charge above station-side specialty pricing if it sells the place well.

## 3. Local pricing tolerance

This section is partly inference, based on the comp set above plus the location's catchment.

### Commuters / locals

Repeat local tolerance is materially lower than tourist tolerance.

My practical ceiling view:

- `¥400-500`: comfortable repeat zone
- `¥500-550`: acceptable if quality is obvious or loyalty mechanics exist
- `¥600+`: occasional local purchase, not reliable repeat behavior for the mass local market

Implication:

- a `¥650` core latte is fine for tourists and occasional locals
- it is too expensive to be the only local repeat anchor
- the menu still needs at least one easy-entry local item or value set

### Students

Students are the most price-sensitive segment and should not be the main Sarusawa volume thesis.

My read:

- likely repeat ceiling: `¥350-450`
- good use case: iced drinks, second-cup offers, off-peak bundles, social / study visits
- bad use case: underwriting the core business on daily student coffee habits

Reason:

- Sarusawa is a tourist-core location, not a campus corridor
- students can add shoulder volume
- they should not be treated as the primary revenue floor

### Weekend local leisure shoppers

This segment is more premium-tolerant than commuters.

Likely workable band:

- `¥500-650` for drinks
- `¥800-1,100` for drink + dessert when atmosphere is strong

This is the best local segment for upselling pastries and seasonal sweets.

## 4. Blended average validation

### User's proposed case

If `70%` of customers are tourists at `¥800` and `30%` are locals at `¥450`, then:

- blended average = `0.70 x ¥800 + 0.30 x ¥450 = ¥695`

That absolutely supports the `¥650-700` range.

### What if tourists are 70% of foot traffic, but convert worse?

This is the more realistic way to frame it.

If tourist foot traffic is `70%`, local foot traffic is `30%`, and tourist conversion is lower than local conversion, then tourist **customer share** falls below `70%`.

Using `¥800` tourist basket and `¥450` local basket:

| Tourist conversion vs local conversion | Tourist share of transactions | Blended average |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| `100%` | `70.0%` | `¥695` |
| `80%` | `65.1%` | `¥678` |
| `60%` | `58.3%` | `¥654` |
| `50%` | `53.8%` | `¥639` |

So your concern is correct, but the result is still encouraging:

- even at only `60%` tourist conversion, the model still lands at about `¥654`

### What if local penetration rises?

Using equal conversion and the same `¥800 / ¥450` ticket assumptions:

| Tourist share of transactions | Local share | Blended average |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| `70%` | `30%` | `¥695` |
| `65%` | `35%` | `¥678` |
| `60%` | `40%` | `¥660` |
| `55%` | `45%` | `¥643` |
| `50%` | `50%` | `¥625` |

Interpretation:

- `¥650` is still realistic with a healthier local base
- below about `55%` tourist customer share, the store starts needing either a higher tourist ticket or a higher local ticket to hold the `¥650` average

### A more realistic blended base case

If you haircut tourist spending slightly and allow stronger local penetration:

- tourists `¥780`
- locals `¥470`
- tourist foot traffic `70%`
- tourist conversion only `60%` of local conversion

Then:

- tourist share of transactions = about `58.3%`
- blended average = about `¥651`

This is why I think `¥650` is the right base underwriting point.

## 5. Menu strategy impact

### Can the proposed menu reach the target basket?

Your proposed ladder is directionally correct:

- basic espresso / core coffee: `¥600`
- specialty single-origin: `¥850`
- pastry bundle: `+¥300`

The main question is attachment rate.

Formula:

- average basket = average beverage price + (`pastry attach rate x ¥300`)

What that means in practice:

| Average beverage ticket | Basket target `¥750` | Basket target `¥800` |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| `¥650` | needs `33%` pastry attach | needs `50%` pastry attach |
| `¥680` | needs `23%` pastry attach | needs `40%` pastry attach |
| `¥700` | needs `17%` pastry attach | needs `33%` pastry attach |
| `¥730` | needs `7%` pastry attach | needs `23%` pastry attach |

Conclusion:

- `¥750` average basket is realistic if beverage mix lands around `¥680-700` or pastry attach gets above about `20-25%`
- `¥800` is possible, but not as a normal base case unless the concept gets strong dessert attachment or sells a lot of premium signatures

### Recommended menu architecture

For Sarusawa, I would underwrite this:

- value-entry coffee / americano: `¥580-650`
- core latte / cappuccino: `¥680-780`
- signature / single-origin / seasonal: `¥820-920`
- pastry / small sweet add-on: `¥320-420`

That ladder is broad enough to protect both tourist yield and local repeatability.

## 6. Dynamic pricing

### Peak season

Yes, Sarusawa can support `¥900+`, but only selectively.

My view:

- keep core menu prices stable
- use `¥880-950` seasonal signatures in cherry blossom, Golden Week, autumn foliage, and event weeks
- do **not** push plain batch coffee or core latte to `¥900+`

The tourist-zone comps support premium signatures at this level. They do not support making the whole menu feel overpriced.

### Off-season

I would not cut the whole menu to `¥500`.

Better approach:

- keep the premium positioning
- use local-targeted winter offers such as `¥500-550` morning coffee, `¥780-880` drink + pastry set, or second-cup / student / commuter mechanics

Blanket discounting is likely to damage the brand more than it helps traffic.

### Time-of-day pricing

I do **not** recommend literal time-of-day list-price changes.

I did not find evidence of current local comps using explicit afternoon premiums, and it would likely feel awkward in a Japan cafe context.

Better version:

- afternoon-only dessert combo
- sunset signature drink
- event-night limited menu

Use mix management, not hard dynamic pricing.

## 7. Price-quantity tradeoff and revenue sensitivity

### Break-even transaction requirement

Using the existing Sarusawa cost structure already modeled in this repo:

- fixed costs: about `¥1.27m / month`
- variable cost ratio: about `32%`

Break-even transactions per day by average ticket:

| Average ticket | Break-even tx/day |
| --- | ---: |
| `¥600` | `103.8` |
| `¥650` | `95.8` |
| `¥700` | `88.9` |
| `¥750` | `83.0` |
| `¥800` | `77.8` |
| `¥850` | `73.2` |

This is the key economic tradeoff:

- `¥650` is realistic from a demand perspective
- but compared with `¥950`, it requires materially more volume

### Scenario summary

| Scenario | Average month revenue | Annual revenue using 2024 Nara seasonality | Average-month operating result |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| `80 tx/day @ ¥650` | `¥1.56m` | `¥18.72m` | `-¥0.21m` |
| `95 tx/day @ ¥650` | `¥1.85m` | `¥22.23m` | `-¥0.01m` |
| `100 tx/day @ ¥650` | `¥1.95m` | `¥23.40m` | `+¥0.06m` |
| `90 tx/day @ ¥700` | `¥1.89m` | `¥22.68m` | `+¥0.02m` |

Meaning:

- `¥650` works if the store can push into the high-`90s` tx/day
- if volume is likely to sit closer to `80-90` tx/day, average ticket probably needs to be closer to `¥700`

| Aug | `¥1.80m` | `-¥0.05m` |
| Sep | `¥1.69m` | `-¥0.12m` |
| Oct | `¥2.17m` | `+¥0.21m` |

Interpretation:

- `¥650` is commercially believable
- but it leaves the business exposed in winter unless transaction volume is stronger than the older `80 tx/day` case
- the cleanest path is either:
  - hold `¥650-680` and drive `95-100 tx/day`, or
  - hold `90 tx/day` and push average ticket closer to `¥700`

## Final judgment

`¥650` is realistic.

More precisely:

- `Yes` as a blended average transaction assumption for Sarusawa Pond
- `Yes` even under a more realistic case where tourists convert below their foot-traffic share
- `No` if it is being used to justify the earlier low-volume economics without a higher transaction count

My recommendation is to underwrite:

- base case: `¥650-680`
- conservative case: `¥620-640`
- upside case: `¥720-780` only if pastry / dessert attachment is intentionally designed into the model

If the business plan still assumes around `80 tx/day`, then `¥650` is too low for the cost base. If the team believes Sarusawa can sustain around `95-100 tx/day`, then `¥650` is a solid and defensible working assumption.
